# LinkedIn Engagement Playbook

> Goal: Get discovered by SMB owners in target verticals without posting original content every day.
> Time budget: 20–30 minutes/day, 5 days/week.

---

## The Core Principle

**You don't need to post to grow on LinkedIn. You need to be seen by the right people.**

Comments on other people's posts show up in the feeds of their followers. If you comment on a post that has 300 likes, your name is visible to thousands — including SMB owners in your target verticals who follow that person.

Posting is a long game. Engagement is a short game. Do both, but don't let the absence of posts stop you from building presence.

---

## Daily Routine (20–30 min)

### Block 1: Warm your feed (5 min)
- Open LinkedIn. Scroll your home feed for 5 minutes.
- Like 5–10 posts from people in your target verticals.
- This trains the algorithm to show you more relevant content.

### Block 2: Comment on 3–5 posts (15 min)
- Find 3–5 posts to comment on (see "Where to Find Posts" below).
- Write one substantive comment per post (see "Comment Formula" below).
- Do NOT comment on posts older than 48 hours — algorithm deprioritises late comments.

### Block 3: Follow up (5 min)
- Reply to any comments on your own posts or previous comments.
- Send 1–2 connection requests to people who engaged with your comments.
- Check DMs and reply if needed.

- `#automation` `#aitools` `#businessautomation` `#workflowautomation`
- `#entrepreneurship` `#businessgrowth` `#salesautomation`

### Priority 3: Accounts to follow and engage with regularly
Follow accounts that consistently post content your target buyers would read:
- Business coaches who work with trades/clinics/fitness
- Local Chamber of Commerce accounts
- "Lessons from running a [gym/practice/trade business]" type creators
- AI/automation consultants (engage thoughtfully — they won't be clients but their followers might be)

## Comment Formula

Bad comment: "Great post! Really resonates."
Good comment: adds value, shows expertise, sparks conversation.

### Formula: **Validate → Add → Question**
1. One sentence that validates what they said (shows you read it)
2. One or two sentences that add a specific insight, example, or data point
3. Optional: end with a question to invite a reply

### Examples

**On a gym owner's post about struggling with admin:**
> Running a gym is 20% training clients and 80% chasing payments, no-shows, and unanswered enquiries. We've seen studios cut 10+ hours/week of admin by automating just 3 things: new member onboarding, class reminders, and follow-ups for free trial leads. Which of those eats most of your time?

**On a tradesperson's post about missing calls while on site:**
> This is the single biggest revenue leak in trades businesses. A missed call at 10am that goes to voicemail is usually a booked job for your competitor by noon. Automating the initial response — even just an instant text with "we got your call, here's when we'll ring back" — changes the conversion rate completely. What do you currently do when you can't answer?

**On a clinic owner's post about no-shows:**
> No-shows are brutal — not just for revenue but for the diary chaos they create. The research is pretty consistent: 2–3 automated reminders (48h, 24h, 2h before) drops no-show rates by 50–70%. Have you tried SMS reminders or is it still email/manual calls?

**On an AI/automation post by a consultant:**
> Good framing. The gap we see most often is businesses that automate the *tool* but not the *workflow* — they set up a chatbot but don't map what happens after a lead says "I'm interested." The handoff to human review is where 80% of leads go cold. What's your approach to designing the post-automation handoff?

## Comment Quality Standards

- **Minimum length:** 2–3 sentences. One-liners don't move the algorithm.
- **No generic phrases:** "Great post", "So true", "This!", "Couldn't agree more" — delete these on sight.
- **No self-promotion:** Don't mention your services or link to your website in comments. Let your profile do that work.
- **Show vertical knowledge:** Reference something specific to their industry. Generic automation comments don't land.
- **Be direct:** State a view. Comments that sit on the fence get no engagement.

## How to Turn Engagement into Conversations

### When someone replies to your comment:
1. Reply back — keep the thread going (1–2 exchanges).
2. If the exchange shows they have the problem you solve, send a connection request with a note.

### Connection request note (after comment exchange):
> Hi [Name], enjoyed the exchange in [person]'s post about [topic]. You clearly know this space well. Happy to connect — I work on AI automation for [vertical] businesses and always good to know people dealing with these problems first-hand.

### After they accept:
Wait 2–3 days, then send one message:
> Thanks for connecting [Name]. No pitch — just curious: is [the problem you discussed] still your main bottleneck, or has something else taken over? Happy to share what we've seen work in other [vertical] businesses if useful.

### If they reply with interest:
Move to discovery call. Use the discovery call script ([Projects/client-onboarding-template.md](client-onboarding-template.md)).

## Weekly Engagement Targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Posts liked/week | 30–50 |
| Substantive comments/week | 15–25 |
| Connection requests sent/week | 5–10 |
| DMs sent (post-connection)/week | 3–5 |
| Discovery calls booked/week | 1+ (realistic by Week 3) |

Start at the low end. Quality over volume — 15 excellent comments beat 40 generic ones.

## Engagement Without Posting: Month 1 Realistic Outcome

- Week 1: Algorithm starts learning. Small profile view uptick.
- Week 2: First connection requests accepted. 1–2 DM conversations started.
- Week 3: Profile views double from Week 1. Possible first discovery call booked.
- Week 4: Clear signal on which verticals are most responsive. Refine targeting.

Expect 0 booked calls in Week 1–2. That's normal. The pipeline is filling.

When a comment you write gets 5+ replies or 20+ likes, it's a post idea.
Take the comment, expand it slightly, and post it as standalone content.
This means your content calendar writes itself from your best engagement.

## ADHD Execution Notes

- **Do this in one block, not scattered.** Open LinkedIn once, do all 3 blocks, close it.
- **Don't browse after finishing.** LinkedIn's feed is designed to eat time. Set a 30-minute timer.
- **Track in a simple spreadsheet:** Date | Post commented on | Person's name | Vertical | Did they reply?
- **Lowest viable version:** If you only have 10 minutes, write 2 comments. That still compounds.
