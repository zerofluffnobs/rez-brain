# Discovery Call Script — 20-Minute First Call

> Use this after a lead replies to an outreach email or messages you on LinkedIn/Contra/Upwork. Goal: qualify pain, surface budget, close to next step. Do NOT pitch features — diagnose problems.

---

## Before the Call (2 min prep)

- [ ] Know the vertical (trades / fitness / clinic / real estate / other)
- [ ] Know how they found you (cold email / LinkedIn / platform / referral)
- [ ] Have the relevant blog post URL ready (send after call if relevant)
- [ ] Have service-packages.md open for pricing reference
- [ ] Have your calendar open to book next step live

---

## Opening — Set the Frame (0:00–2:00)

> Goal: be warm, establish you're running the call, make them feel heard.

**Script:**

"Hey [Name], great to connect — thanks for making the time.

I've got us blocked for about 20 minutes. What I'd like to do is spend most of it understanding what's going on in your business, and then if it sounds like I can genuinely help, I'll tell you what that looks like. Does that work?

Great. So tell me — what's happening that made you reach out?"

**Then shut up.** Let them talk. Do not interrupt.

---

## Pain Qualification — Dig Deep (2:00–10:00)

> Goal: get to the real problem, not the surface symptom. Ask until you hit pain that has a cost.

### Round 1 — What's the problem?

"What does that cost you, day to day?"

"How long has this been going on?"

"Has anything changed recently that made it more urgent?"

### Round 2 — What's it actually costing?

"If I'm doing the math — how many hours a week does [the problem] eat up?"

"And who's doing that work — is it you, or are you paying someone?"

"So roughly what does that work out to per month?"

*(If they don't know: "It's fine to ballpark — even a rough number helps me understand the scale.")*

### Round 3 — What have they tried?

"Have you looked at fixing this before? What got in the way?"

"Are there any tools you're already using that you like — things I'd need to work around?"

### Round 4 — Why now?

"What made you decide to look at this now rather than six months ago?"

*(This surfaces urgency and helps you understand their decision timeline.)*

---

## Fit Check — Do They Qualify? (10:00–13:00)

> Goal: confirm they're a real prospect (pain + budget + decision-making ability).

### Decision-maker check

"When you're thinking about investing in something like this — is that a decision you can make yourself, or does someone else need to be involved?"

*(If yes: "Great." If no: "Would it make sense to have [that person] on the next call?")*

### Budget surface (do this gently)

"I want to make sure we're in the same ballpark before I put anything together. Depending on scope, projects like this typically run from around €500–600 for a focused chatbot build, up to €1,200–1,500 for a full automation system. Does that range feel workable, or is there a ceiling I should know about?"

**Listen for:**
- "Yes that's fine" → green light
- "That seems high" → don't discount yet, anchor to their cost ("You said it's costing you €800/month…")
- "We have budget but need approval" → find out process
- "No, we can't do that" → politely qualify out, offer a referral

### Quick fit check

"Based on what you've told me — I think there's a real fit here. The [trades / fitness / clinic] businesses I've worked with had a very similar setup. Let me tell you what I'd typically do."

---

## Solution Sketch — Brief, Not a Pitch (13:00–16:00)

> Goal: give them enough to be excited, not enough to DIY it. One concrete example, not a feature list.

"What I'd probably build for you is [X]. It would handle [Y] automatically, so [Z] stops happening.

For a gym that had a similar problem, this saved them about 10 hours a week and cut no-shows by around 30%.

I'd put together a short one-page proposal based on what you've told me — that would include the scope, the cost, and a timeline."

**Do NOT go into tech stack, APIs, or architecture.** They don't care.

---

## Close to Next Step (16:00–19:00)

> Goal: book a specific next action before you hang up. Vague "I'll send something over" = ghost risk.

### If qualified:

"Here's what I'd suggest: I'll put together a one-pager based on what we talked about — you'll have it within [24/48 hours]. Then we jump on a second call — 15 minutes — to walk through it and answer any questions. Does that work?

What does your calendar look like [day after tomorrow / early next week]?"

*(Book it live. Don't leave it to email back-and-forth.)*

### If not yet qualified (decision-maker missing):

"I'd love to put together something concrete for you. Would it be worth getting [partner/boss] on a quick call so I can understand the full picture before I write anything up?"

### If budget is unclear:

"Let me put together two options — one focused, one more comprehensive — so you can see what's possible at different investment levels. I'll have that to you by [date]."

---

## Wrap (19:00–20:00)

"Perfect. So: I'll send the proposal over by [date], and we'll talk again [booked time]. Any questions before I let you go?

Great — looking forward to it."

---

## After the Call (within 2 hours)

Send the follow-up email from `outreach-templates.md` (Touch 1 replacement — use the "post-call" variant):

> Subject: Great speaking — here's what I'll put together

> "Hey [Name], really enjoyed the chat. As promised, I'll have your proposal over by [date]. In the meantime, here's a quick read that might be useful: [relevant blog post link]. Talk [booked date]."

---

## Red Flags — Qualify Out Gracefully

| Signal | What to Say |
|---|---|
| No budget and no timeline | "It sounds like the timing isn't quite right. Mind if I check back in a couple of months?" |
| Wants to DIY with your help | "I focus on full builds rather than consulting — might not be the best fit, but happy to point you somewhere." |
| Wants to start immediately but can't pay | "I need a deposit before I block time. Let me know when you're ready." |
| Multiple decision-makers, no timeline | "Get me in front of the room — I'll do the pitch. Otherwise it tends to drag." |

## Quick Reference Card (print/phone)

```
OPEN:    "Tell me what's happening."
COST:    "How many hours? Who does it? What's that per month?"
TRIED:   "What have you tried before?"
URGENCY: "Why now?"
BUDGET:  "€500–1,500 range — workable?"
CLOSE:   Book next call before hanging up.
```
