# Email Nurture Sequence

> Follow-up cadence for cold outreach non-responders. 5 touches over 30 days.
> Pair with: Projects/outreach-templates.md (cold email #1), Projects/launch-checklist.md

## Why This Exists

The cold email gets sent. Then what? Most freelancers send one email and give up. The data says 80% of sales happen on follow-up #2–#5. This sequence gives you a full 30-day nurture cadence for each lead — without being annoying.

**The rule:** each follow-up adds value. Never just "checking in."

---

## Universal Follow-Up Logic

| Touch | Day | Subject | Angle |
|-------|-----|---------|-------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Cold email (see outreach-templates.md) | Pain + curiosity |
| 2 | Day 3 | Short bump + relevant stat | Social proof / urgency |
| 3 | Day 7 | Value drop (blog post / demo link) | Helpfulness |
| 4 | Day 14 | Case study / before-after | Credibility |
| 5 | Day 30 | Break-up + referral ask | Closes the loop |

## Execution Notes (ADHD-Friendly)

### Tracking Setup
Use a simple spreadsheet or Notion board. Columns:
```
Name | Business | Email | Sent Date | T2 Date | T3 Date | T4 Date | T5 Date | Status
```

Status options: `cold → touched-2 → touched-3 → touched-4 → closed-out → replied → booked → won → lost`

### Calendar Rule
When you send Touch 1, immediately create calendar reminders for:
- T2: +3 days
- T3: +7 days
- T4: +14 days
- T5: +30 days

### If They Reply at Any Point
Stop the sequence. Move to live conversation. Reply within 2 hours max — you just beat the speed-to-lead problem.

### Personalisation Checklist (per email)
- [ ] First name in subject or opener
- [ ] Business name mentioned at least once
- [ ] Vertical-specific stat used (not generic)
- [ ] CTA is one clear action (not multiple options)

### Volume
Start with 5 leads/week. At 5 leads × 5 touches = 25 emails/month managed. Trackable without a CRM. Scale up once replies start coming in.
